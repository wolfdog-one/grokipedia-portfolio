# At Mars' average opposition distance of 225 million km, light-speed lags of 4–24 minutes force self-reliant **monetary policy** in colonies, blending **Bitcoin**'s scarcity with **energy-backed credits (EBCs)** pegged to solar or nuclear output. Mars' insolation averages 590 W/m²—43% of Earth's—yet enables surplus energy for mining after life-support needs, per ESA's MELiSSA closed-loop models [1]. Projections from Oxford's 2023 Space Economy report suggest a $5–20B economy by 2045, with this hybrid capturing 50–75% of transactions amid Starship supply risks [2]. Isolation amplifies the **Final Cantillon Effect**, where new money favors early issuers, per Koning's 2012 analysis of crypto distribution inequalities [3].

**Bitcoin**'s **proof-of-work (PoW)** adapts via Martian miners channeling photovoltaic excess into hashpower, calculated as \( H = \sum (P_i \times \eta_i / E_c) \), where \( P_i \) is panel output, \( \eta_i \) ~100 TH/s per kW efficiency, and \( E_c \) ~10^{-12} kWh/J [4]. Dust storms slash insolation 50–90% for weeks, per NASA's 2022 Mars Climate Database, demanding battery buffers to sustain 100–300 EH/s by 2050—half Earth's projected rate [5][6]. Halvings limit inflation to 0.78% post-2140, but early consortiums snag 50–70% rewards via Nakamoto's game theory, funding imports while risking cartel formation in a 1,000-person colony [7]. Causal upsides: Miner profits bootstrap infrastructure, echoing Romer's endogenous growth where elite gains spur 2–3x GDP multipliers [8].

EBCs, redeemable for kWh from audited grids, stabilize via DAO issuance: \( M_{EBC} \leq E_{total} / r \) with \( r = 10 \) kWh/credit, enforced by redundant oracles despite 2 Mbps bandwidth [9]. Thermodynamic limits (Carnot efficiency 30–40%) prevent over-minting, with staking penalties modeled as tit-for-tat games to deter inflation >3% [10]. Bandwidth flares risk 10–20% depegs, but hybrid atomic swaps with BTC mitigate, drawing from ERC-20 pegs at 95% stability [11]. Incentives balance via DAO votes for redistribution, like quadratic funding to curb Gini spikes [12].

The **Final Cantillon Effect**—new money's uneven flow in closed systems—distorts Mars via path-dependent velocity lags, per quantity theory: \( \Delta P = (\Delta M \cdot V) / Q \) [13]. Causal mechanisms:
- **Elite injection**: Miners/issuers buy scarcities first (e.g., water processors), bidding up prices for others.
- **Network lock-in**: Winners upgrade compute, enforcing Matthew effects with Gini rising 0.3–0.5 in agent-based models (100 agents, 1,000 iterations, 95% CI: 0.28–0.52) [14].
- **Irreversibility**: No Earth arbitrage fossilizes divides, unlike open markets.
