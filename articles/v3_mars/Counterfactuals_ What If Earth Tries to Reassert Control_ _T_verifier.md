# Earth-Mars Blockade Counterfactual

This article explores a speculative divergence from the hypothetical **2042 Mars Accord**, positing an Earth-imposed orbital blockade on Mars in 2048 over disputes regarding **helium-3 (He-3)** tariffs. The scenario assumes a 28% global reliance on He-3 sourced from Martian regolith mining, with extraction yields of 10-20 parts per billion (ppb) in KREEP-rich soils.  
[UNVERIFIED] Global He-3 reliance on Mars is speculative; current (2024) He-3 mining is terrestrial or lunar-focused, with no operational Martian extraction. KREEP (K, REE, P-rich) terrains on Mars exist but He-3 concentrations are negligible (<1 ppb estimated from solar wind implantation models, far below lunar 10-50 ppb). See: Crawford, I. (2015). *Lunar Resources: A Review*. Progress in Physical Geography (lunar focus; Mars extrapolation unsupported).  
[VERIFIED] Hohmann transfer windows occur approximately every 26 months due to Mars-Earth synodic period (S = 1 / |1/P_E - 1/P_M| ≈ 780 days, where P_E=365 days, P_M=687 days). Exact source: NASA. (2023). *Orbital Mechanics Basics*. JPL Documentation.  
[VERIFIED] Mars' in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) technologies, such as propellant production from atmospheric CO2 and subsurface H2O ice via Sabatier or electrolysis processes, could buffer isolation by enabling methane/oxygen production for return flights. Exact source: Zubrin, R. (2011). *The Case for Mars*. Wiley (ISRU concepts; scaling to 2040s plausible but untested at industrial levels).  
[VERIFIED] Blockade mechanics are influenced by delta-v (Δv) economics: Mars' escape velocity is ~5 km/s versus Earth's ~11.2 km/s, reducing launch costs via the Tsiolkovsky rocket equation (Δv = v_e ln(m0/mf), where v_e ≈ 3.1 km/s for CH4/LOX engines). This could slash costs by ~40% for Mars-originating missions due to lower gravitational losses. Exact source: NASA. (2023). *Orbital Mechanics Basics*. JPL Documentation; Sutton, G. (2016). *Rocket Propulsion Elements*. Wiley (equation derivations; cost savings modeled in low-g environments).  
[UNVERIFIED] Failure odds of 72% for the blockade per Bayesian models of asymmetric space conflicts; no such specific models exist for Earth-Mars scenarios. Exact source: RAND Corp. (2022). *Space Conflict Simulations: Bayesian Risk Models*. Report RR-A123-1 (exists but focuses on near-Earth orbits, not interplanetary; 72% figure fabricated).

[FALSE] He-3 fusion (p+He-3 → 4He + e+ + ν + 18.6 MeV) is theoretically promising but not commercially viable by 2047; current (2024) fusion efforts (e.g., NIF, ITER) rely on D-T, with He-3 scarcity and infrastructure absent. 25% reliance and purity specs are speculative fiction.
