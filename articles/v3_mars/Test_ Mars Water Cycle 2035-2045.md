# Mechanistic Drivers

Sublimation flux follows \( J = \alpha \frac{P_{sat}(T) - P_v}{\sqrt{2\pi M / RT}} \), tweaked for 0.38g low gravity, peaking at southern summer (Ls=270°) with insolation spikes from 25.2° **obliquity**[4]. Dust storms turbocharge transport, lofting vapor 10-20 km but redepositing 90% locally per GCM models[5]. Briny perchlorates enable **deliquescence** at RH>60% and T>-20°C, birthing transient flows like recurring slope lineae (RSL)[6]. Escape rates could dip 20-40% under solar minimum, but solar wind stripping laughs off weak shields[7].

- **Natural Variability**: Obliquity wobbles (120ky cycle) drive 2-5x ice loss swings; dust opacity triples vapor mixing[5].
- **Anthropogenic Tweaks**: ISRU drills (e.g., MOXIE analogs) might release 1-10 kg/day H₂O, boosting dome RH by <1% globally—negligible without leaks[8].
- **Uncertainties**: D/H ratio (~6x Earth) tracks pristine history; contamination risks skew isotopes by 10-20% in alt-scenarios[9].
